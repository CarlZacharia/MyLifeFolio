— I.

Getting started

What is MyLifeFolio?

MyLifeFolio is a secure digital life plan that organizes your personal information, finances, healthcare wishes, documents, family contacts, and legacy in one place — and gives the people you trust controlled access to exactly the right information at exactly the right moment.

Who is MyLifeFolio for?

MyLifeFolio is for anyone who wants their family to know what to do in an emergency, during a medical event, or after a loss. It is especially valuable for parents, caregivers, retirees, and anyone with an estate plan who wants the practical information their family will actually need — account numbers, providers, wishes, passwords, documents — organized alongside the legal documents themselves.

How long does it take to set up?

You can get something useful in 30 minutes by completing the foundational categories (personal information, key contacts, basic financial inventory). A complete folio with all categories typically takes 3–5 hours spread across several sittings — and you can return at any time to add or refine.

— II.

Pricing & plans

How much does MyLifeFolio cost?

MyLifeFolio is $149 per year. Start with a 6-month free trial — no credit card required up front. Add a payment method only if you decide to keep going at the 6-month mark.

Why no credit card up front?

We do not want anything in the way of you trying it. Add a card only if you decide to keep going at the 6-month mark.

What happens to my data if I do not renew?

It is permanently deleted. We do not store inactive accounts. You will get four reminder emails — 30 days before the trial ends, 7 days before, the day of, and 7 days after — so you have plenty of warning to renew or to download anything you want to keep.

Can I cancel partway through the year?

Yes. Your subscription stays active through the end of the period you paid for, then deletes per the same policy as a trial cancellation.

Can I download my data before deletion?

Yes. You can export a complete copy of your folio at any time, including documents from the vault. We also include the export reminder in the deletion warning emails so you do not lose anything by accident.

— III.

Security & data

Is my data secure?

All data is encrypted at rest. The credential vault uses client-side AES-256 encryption — even MyLifeFolio cannot read it. Row-level security ensures only you and the people you grant access to can see your folio. See the full Security model.

Can MyLifeFolio read my passwords or credentials?

No. The credential vault is encrypted on your device with AES-256 before any data leaves your browser. The encryption key is derived from your passphrase and never sent to us. We see only encrypted bytes — there is no MyLifeFolio employee, support agent, or administrator who can decrypt your credentials.

Where is my data stored?

On Supabase infrastructure with strict row-level-security policies that scope every record to its owner. All connections use TLS in transit and AES-256 at rest.

Is MyLifeFolio HIPAA compliant?

MyLifeFolio is a personal record-keeping platform owned and controlled by you, not a healthcare provider, so HIPAA does not directly apply. We follow strong security practices — AES-256 vault encryption, row-level security, encryption at rest — appropriate for the sensitive nature of the information stored.

— IV.

Family access

How does family access work?

You decide who gets access, what they can see, and when. Family members log in through a separate Family Access portal and only see the categories you have specifically granted to them.

What if I am incapacitated and cannot grant access myself?

Family access can be configured in advance so that designated people are pre-approved for specific information categories. You can also share access with your attorney or trusted contact who can coordinate during an emergency.

Can I revoke access?

Yes, at any time. You control who has access and to what — and you can change those grants whenever you need to.

What does a family member actually see when they log in?

Only the categories you have granted to them. The Family Access portal is a read-only experience designed to surface the practical information they need — provider names, document copies, contact information, your wishes — without exposing categories you have not shared.

— V.

What it organizes

What categories of information does MyLifeFolio organize?

Personal and family information, health and medical, financial life, estate plan and legal documents, document vault, end-of-life and care preferences, legacy and life story, and digital life. You enable only the categories you want to use.

Can I upload documents like my will, deed, or insurance policies?

Yes. The Document Vault stores your important documents with the same encryption and controlled-access model as the rest of the folio. You can grant specific people access to specific documents.

Does it handle digital assets and online accounts?

Yes. The Digital Life category captures your online accounts, social media, and digital subscriptions. Credentials live in the AES-256 client-side vault.

— VI.

Features & AI

What is included in the AI obituary?

An AI-generated, thoughtful obituary draft based on the life information you have entered. You can generate multiple versions and edit them to your liking. Included for everyone — trial and paid alike.

Are there other AI-assisted features?

Yes. AI helps with drafting personal letters, generating reflection prompts, and structuring legacy content. Your information is used to personalize the output for you and is not used to train external models.

— VII.

Fit with your existing plan

How is MyLifeFolio different from a will or estate plan?

An estate plan is the legal framework. MyLifeFolio is the operational guide — the inventory of accounts, the medical directives, the family contacts, the digital assets, and the personal wishes that families actually need to find and use during a crisis. The two work together.

Does MyLifeFolio replace my attorney or financial advisor?

No. MyLifeFolio organizes information about the work your attorneys and advisors have done — and the personal context they cannot see. It is designed to make their work more useful to your family, not to replace it.

I already have a will. Why do I still need this?

Because a will tells your family what you want to give them — but not where to find anything, who to call, what your wishes are for care, what your account numbers are, or what your story is. MyLifeFolio fills the operational gap that the legal documents alone leave behind.
