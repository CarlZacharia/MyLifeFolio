The gap you have seen a hundred times

The estate plan is signed. The binder goes home with the client. Two years later, three years later, sometimes a decade later, the family calls — and they cannot find anything. They do not know which bank holds the IRA. They do not know who the cardiologist is. They do not know the password to the email account that is receiving every important notification. They do not know what their parent would have wanted at the end.

The legal work was right. The operational layer was missing.

"The real problem families face is not legal — it is informational chaos under stress. MyLifeFolio is the answer they will not have to search for."
— II.

How MyLifeFolio fits alongside the work you do

MyLifeFolio is not legal advice and does not replace anything in your engagement. It is a structured platform for the client to record — and selectively share — the operational information that sits adjacent to your legal documents.

What you provide

  • The will, trust, and ancillary documents
  • Powers of attorney (financial & healthcare)
  • Advance directives
  • Counsel on titling, beneficiaries, asset protection
  • The legal architecture of the plan

What MyLifeFolio adds

  • Account inventory (financial institutions, balances, contacts)
  • Healthcare providers, medications, allergies
  • Document vault for copies of your originals
  • Personal wishes, end-of-life preferences, legacy content
  • Granular family access controls
  • Digital assets and credential vault
— III.

Why introduce it to clients

It makes your plan more usable

The hardest call to take is the one from a grieving family who has the documents but cannot find the assets. When clients build a folio alongside the plan, the operational gap closes. Your plan becomes the framework; the folio becomes the map.

It surfaces the conversations that the engagement does not

Filling out a folio prompts clients to think about end-of-life preferences, family communication, legacy, and digital assets — topics that often surface follow-up legal needs (gifting decisions, beneficiary updates, supplemental directives) that benefit your client and your practice.

It positions your practice as forward-looking

Families remember which attorney introduced them to a tool that actually helped them through the hardest week of their life.
